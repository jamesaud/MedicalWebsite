Value Based Care

By 2020 private payers expect 80% of all reimbursements to be VBC. We believe this regulation is a huge improvement over managed care.
VBC provides reimbursements based on "value, not volume", which correlates to quality of care over quantity of care. We strongly believe in quality of care for patients, but also optimize for volume.
Legacy systems, including the industry leaders such as Cerner, Greenway, Nextgen, & Epic have reduced productivity up to 30%. Emerging systems like Synergy+ calculate time vs cost of care, as well as different treatment methods, in order to discern the best approach for VBC.

Critical EHR Interoperability Update

What you choose to do now will affect whether you can stay independent in the future. Over 65% of existing EHRs must be replaced by 2020 - those not up to date are at risk for high fees, including long-term labor costs due to incompatible EHR interoperability. Interoperability in essential for VBC reimbursements.
The bad news is that you likely have to replace your EHR to compensate. Extra IT staff, support staff, and vendor fees are required for old systems; maintaining an outdated system is rarely worth the effort.
New vendors like Synergy+ provide EHR's that are built around Interoperability. Upgrading to a new system now saves time and money in the future.

Chronic Care

Over 86% of all healthcare spending is for people with chronic diseases. America has one of the highest rates of chronic disease in the world at over 50%, according to the CDC. Therefore, curing and reducing symptoms of chronic disease is a top priority.
Preventative & self care are the most effective ways to deal with chronic diseases. There is no sure solution; however, Patient Engagement (PE) and PHM are provenly effective in motivating patients to practice preventative and self care. We provide low-cost proven methods for your doctors to approve.
Treating chronic disease is crucial in optimizing VBC reimbursements.

   Frequently Asked Questions

Why are all-inclusive services becoming mandatory?

It is becoming mandatory to utilize all services to optimize patient engagement and reimbursements. Few can afford to buy separate services, as the time and cost to deal with multiple vendors is inefficient and the services are not integrated.

Population health management is essential for Value Based Care (VBC). VBC is soon to account for up to 30% of all patients, and Private Pay will be 80% of VBC by 2020.

What is cloud based EHR software?

Cloud computing delivers information as a service (IaaS) rather than a product. Resources, software, and information are provided to computers over a network. End users access cloud-based applications through a web browser, a lightweight desktop or mobile app. Cloud application provides a higher performance level than if the software programs were installed locally on end-user computers.

Hardware breaks down and software gets corrupted. Older, outdated EHR (electronic health records) systems rely on expensive and often unstable hardware and software. Unfortunately when hardware breaks down or software gets corrupted, it can shut down an entire office, not to mention the potential for lost data and records. Being a cloud-based system means NovoMedici eliminates the need for proprietary hardware and software.

Best yet, cloud-based means the system is safe, easily expandable, and data is constantly being backed up at an offsite location.

How do I transition from my current system?

There are two approaches, depending on your practice and current system.

Instant transition is a great approach for small practices, where we work with you over 1-2 days on a weekend to transition from your current system to our system. Everything is up and running by Monday, so you are ready to see people.

Another approach, which is recommended for larger practices, is gradually shifting to the new system. This can be done over the course of a few weeks, where a few doctors at a time integrate into the new system.

All electronic and physical records are transfered to our system, which can be be quite quick and cost-efficient if your system has optimal interoperability.

How do your technology and services optimize patient care?

Instead of thinking of healthcare as isolated components, we approach it as an complex system. Consider our treatment approach for chronic care. Doctors spend 8-10 minutes more per patient because our EHR & RCM work asynchronously with eachother; the RCM automatically codes and bills as you're filling out patient information.

PE is a mix of technology and services to optimize patient visits, which helps increase patient compliance & chronic care outcomes. PHM and Data Analytics provide optimal treatment methods for patients, further helping treat chronic care.

Furthermore, our all-inclusive services help you focus on treating patients and not worry about non-clinical issues: we find you doctors, provide consulting, inform you with monthly research reports, and more.
